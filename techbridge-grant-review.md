# TechBridge Grant Application Review

## Critical review of draft submission against TechLocal competition requirements and assessor scoring guidance.

---

## CRITICAL / POTENTIALLY DISQUALIFYING ISSUES

### 1. Total project costs may exceed the competition cap
**Issue:** The competition states "Project Size: £100,000-£225,000 per project." The draft claims £285,714 total eligible costs. If "project size" means total eligible costs (which is the standard Innovate UK interpretation), the application is non-compliant and will be rejected before assessment.
**Action:** Verify urgently with IUK support (0300 321 4357) whether the £225k cap is on total eligible costs or grant amount. If it's total costs, you must either (a) reduce total costs to £225k (grant ~£157.5k at 70%) or (b) reframe the budget. If it's grant amount, the £200k request is fine.

### 2. Job numbers put you in the 7-8 scoring band, not 9-10
**Issue:** The assessor guidance for Q12 is explicit:
- **Scores 9-10:** "The project is aiming to deliver 50 or more jobs" and "support 42 or more women"
- **Scores 7-8:** "25 and 50 jobs" and "10 and 25 women"

The draft targets **35 jobs** and **18 women**. This places you firmly in the **7-8 band**. The guidance also states: "Projects with the highest number of jobs created and support for women into jobs will be prioritised in the scoring." This is a direct ranking signal, not just a threshold.

**Action:** Increase the target to **50 jobs** and **25 women** if you can credibly deliver. Even 45 would be closer to the top band. If 35 is genuinely the maximum, you need an exceptionally strong narrative elsewhere, but know you are conceding points on the single most explicitly weighted criterion. Consider whether widening employer engagement (beyond Cornwall/Devon into broader South West) or adding more candidate pipeline sources could support a higher number.

### 3. Several answers significantly exceed the 400-word limit
**Issue:** The Scope, Q10 (Approach), and Q14 (Project Management) sections are all flagged as "TOO MANY WORDS." IUK's system will likely hard-reject text beyond the word limit.
**Action:** Every scored answer must be ruthlessly edited to 400 words or fewer. The non-scored Scope section also has a 400-word limit.

### 4. No appendices mentioned
**Issue:** The competition allows appendices for Q10 (approach), Q11 (team), Q14 (project management - appendix is **mandatory**: "You must submit a project plan or Gantt chart"), and Q15 (risks - appendix is **mandatory**: "You must submit a risk register"). The draft makes no mention of preparing any appendices.
**Action:** You **must** prepare:
- Q14: A project plan / Gantt chart (mandatory, PDF, up to 2 A4 pages)
- Q15: A risk register (mandatory, PDF, up to 2 A4 pages)

You **should** also prepare:
- Q10: An approach diagram showing the funnel from employer engagement through to 6-month retention
- Q11: Key personnel summaries (biographies/CVs for Ruth, Josh, Alex)

These appendices are a huge opportunity to convey detail that won't fit in 400 words.

## CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES

### A. Alvia's platform capability is undersold throughout
The draft describes Alvia generically as "AI-powered qualitative research platform" with "voice interviewing, persona simulation, and hierarchical analytics." An assessor unfamiliar with Alvia won't understand why this is different from a survey tool or a chatbot. In 1-2 sentences (reusable across questions), explain what makes Alvia's approach distinctive:
- Voice interviews that surface qualitative signals traditional surveys and forms miss
- Persona simulation that allows pre-testing and refinement before engaging real employers/candidates
- Hierarchical analytics that aggregate insights across interviews to identify patterns invisible in individual conversations

This should appear prominently in Q10 (Approach) and Q8 (Frontier Technologies).

### B. The "innovation" framing needs work
TechLocal isn't primarily an innovation competition — it's a jobs-and-skills programme. But the assessor guidance for Q10 still scores for "significantly innovative." The draft's innovation is real (using scalable qualitative AI to reduce entry-level hiring friction) but it's presented as a process, not an innovation. Reframe: the innovation is that structured qualitative intelligence at scale has never been applied to the training-to-employment conversion problem before. Traditional approaches rely on CVs, job descriptions, and recruiter judgement. This approach captures the hidden signals that drive real hiring decisions.

### C. Gender equality approach lacks specificity
"50% of starts for women" appears multiple times but the mechanisms are generic ("targeted outreach," "employer-side improvements," "gender-disaggregated insights"). Assessors will want to know:
- Which specific outreach channels reach women? (TECwomen is mentioned once in Q11 — bring it forward)
- What specific changes to recruitment practice will you recommend to employers based on gendered findings?
- Will interview templates include gender-bias detection?
- How will you track and report the women-in-tech KPI during the project, not just at the end?

### D. No mention of alignment with DWP Jobcentres or Growth and Skills Levy in Q9
The assessor guidance for Q9 specifically asks about alignment with "local economic growth plans, skills strategies, local skills improvement plans, DWP job centres, the Growth and Skills Levy and local defence plans." The draft mentions Jobcentres and GSL as candidate pipeline sources (Q10, Q11) but does not discuss strategic alignment with these programmes in Q9 where the assessor is looking for it. Add 1-2 sentences in Q9 showing how TechBridge complements (not duplicates) DWP and GSL provision.

### E. Typo in application location note
"Needs Alivia Biz Registered address" — "Alivia" should be "Alvia."

### F. The 6-month job sustainability requirement creates a timeline crunch
If the project runs July 2026 to March 2027 (9 months), and jobs must sustain for 6 months at 25+ hrs/week, then:
- A hire starting in October 2026 won't reach 6 months until April 2027 — after the project ends
- The draft addresses this with "post-project monitoring at no grant cost" which is good
- But the assessor may question how many of the 35 jobs can actually be evidenced within the funded period
- Consider explicitly stating how many hires you expect to evidence within the project period vs during post-project monitoring, and confirming the monitoring is resourced

### G. No URLs allowed in the application
The competition rules state: "Applications cannot include website addresses (URLs); inclusion causes ineligibility." Double-check all final text for any URLs before submitting.
