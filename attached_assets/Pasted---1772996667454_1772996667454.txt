  Concrete Prompt Change Proposal                                                                                           
                                                                                                                            
  Here's my review of the seven options mapped to specific lines in the codebase, with a recommended adoption plan.         
                  
  Summary verdict

  I'd recommend adopting A + B + C + D + F as a coherent bundle, and deferring E and G (they're partially redundant with the
   others).

  ---
  1. Option A — Lower the "good enough" bar (Barbara system prompt)

  File: server/barbara-orchestrator.ts
  Line 383, currently:
  5. Decide whether the current question now appears sufficiently covered.
     If so, prefer suggesting movement rather than repeating the topic.

  Change to:
  5. Decide whether the current question is sufficiently covered.
     "Sufficiently covered" means: (a) the core intent of the question has been answered,
     (b) at least one concrete supporting detail or example was given, and
     (c) no major contradiction remains unresolved.
     Once these three conditions are met, prefer "suggest_next_question" over further probing.

  Rationale: This is the highest-impact single change. It gives Barbara a concrete, testable definition instead of leaving
  "sufficiently covered" to interpretation — which currently biases toward thoroughness.

  ---
  2. Option B — Reweight priority order (Barbara system prompt)

  File: server/barbara-orchestrator.ts
  Line 719, currently:
  Optimise for continuity first (when bridging genuinely helps), then for completeness, then for efficiency.

  Change to:
  Optimise for continuity first (when bridging genuinely helps), then for efficiency (avoid repetition and maintain
  momentum), then for completeness. In spoken interviews, prefer momentum once the key objective of the question is met.

  Also update line 379 (DECISION ORDER header area) to match — currently the implicit ordering in the numbered steps already
   puts continuity first, but adding a sentence after step 4:

  4a. In spoken interviews, momentum matters. Once the core objective is answered with supporting detail, default toward
  progression rather than exhaustive coverage.

  Rationale: Completeness being second in priority is the root cause of dwelling. Demoting it below efficiency directly
  addresses the issue without removing it entirely.

  ---
  3. Option C — Relax conservative-intervention sentence (Barbara system prompt)

  File: server/barbara-orchestrator.ts
  Line 419, currently:
  Be conservative — only intervene when there's a clear benefit. Most of the time, "none" is appropriate.

  Change to:
  Be conservative — only intervene when there's a clear benefit. Use "none" when no intervention would help, but actively
  recommend "suggest_next_question" when further probing would likely repeat what has already been covered. Momentum is
  itself a benefit.

  Rationale: The current "most of the time none" phrasing suppresses suggest_next_question just as much as it suppresses
  unnecessary probes. The revised version keeps the conservative posture for probing while explicitly carving out move-on as
   a valid active recommendation.

  ---
  4. Option D — Tighten move-on trigger for Alvia

  File: server/voice-interview/instructions.ts
  Line 174 (instruction #8), currently:
  8. If the answer is complete, or Barbara suggests moving on, say "Thank you for that answer"
     and signal that they can use the Next Question button below when ready.

  Change to:
  8. If the respondent has answered the core question and provided at least one concrete detail
     or example, do not add another probe by default. Thank them for their answer and let them
     know they can use the Next Question button below when ready. Barbara may also explicitly
     suggest moving on.

  Rationale: This gives Alvia its own "good enough" heuristic matching Option A's definition, so Alvia doesn't depend
  entirely on Barbara signalling to move on. This addresses the lag issue where Barbara's guidance arrives a turn late.

  ---
  5. Option F — Soften burden from question guidance (Alvia prompt)

  File: server/voice-interview/instructions.ts
  Line 170 (instruction #4), currently:
  4. Use the STEER FOR THIS QUESTION to judge the depth needed.
     This is a voice conversation, so use judgment rather than expecting a perfect or exhaustive answer.

  Change to:
  4. Use the STEER FOR THIS QUESTION as directional guidance, not a checklist.
     Do not attempt exhaustive coverage of every guidance point in one question unless the
     respondent naturally expands. This is a voice conversation — good-enough depth with
     natural flow is better than comprehensive coverage that stalls momentum.

  Also apply the same language to the resume/refresh paths in buildSharedContextBlock around line 352 where the STEER block
  is emitted — though the STEER label itself is just the content, the instructions that reference it in the
  resume/continuation blocks (lines 429, 457) already say "Ask follow-ups only when the answer is brief, unclear,
  contradictory, or still missing something important" which is already well-aligned.

  Rationale: The current "use judgment" is too vague and Alvia interprets it as "try to satisfy the guidance." Making it
  explicit that guidance is directional, not a checklist, directly prevents the completionist behaviour.

  ---
  6. Options E and G — Defer

  Option E (anti-dwelling clause) is partially redundant with D+F combined. If D and F don't fully resolve dwelling after
  testing, E can be added as a reinforcement. Adding it now risks over-constraining Alvia and producing interviews that feel
   rushed.

  Option G (follow-up depth is a ceiling) is already partially covered by lines 386-390 in Barbara's prompt. The existing
  language says "prefer suggest_next_question over probe_followup" when at/above depth and "only suggest probing if clearly
  incomplete" when 1 below. Strengthening this further risks making the recommendedFollowUps field a hard cutoff, which
  researchers may not want.
